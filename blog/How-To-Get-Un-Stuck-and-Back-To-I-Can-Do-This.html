Thank you for sharing how you are feeling. We all feel sometimes like we are really stuck, like we can't. It's just a feeling, it will come and go. It doesn't mean you actually can't do something just because you feel like you can't.

Struggling and trying but not succeeding at certain points IS part of the process, not a sign of anything being wrong. The fact that you haven’t succeeded yet in figuring this part out, just means you haven’t succeeded yet. That’s the case with everything we haven’t solved… yet. Every problem is unsolved, until it’s solved. The past doesn't define the future.

In your own time, where the habits are concerned, give a thought to the following three options:

4) Consider doing a mind-body scan right when you feel the urge to stray from the intention you had (or when are doing the behavior you feel like you “can’t” stop).  You don’t necessarily have to stop yourself, but just taking stock of your state in that moment can help you understand what is going on in your mind that is translating to your body in this situation. 

5) Remind yourself (out loud if you’re up for it!) that you don't have to do anything you don’t want to do. You don’t even have to do the habits! If the habits have become a rule rather than a choice, they have become about restriction instead of freedom. So, aside from some possible outside emotional influences, what is really going on here may a simple rebellion. This happens a lot to people. The habits are meant to bring you freedom, simplicity, happiness, and comfort, not stress, pain, and misery. If that is happening, then there is something not right, and we look at how to fix it, okay? No big deal.

If you don’t want to do this anymore, you can stop. Make sure you still want to change, and if you do, let’s look at Step 6.

6) When you feel like you “can’t”, that is an indication that the habit needs to be “Goldilocksed”. Customize the change you are trying to make; it needs to fit you better. When you feel like you can do it, and you’re highly confident you are up to it, that’s the “just right” habit version for you. If you are learning that three meals a day doesn’t work for you, you could try four instead. Lots of people do four a day. Or you could start with not snacking between breakfast and lunch. Or you could start with not snacking for one hour after lunch. You are worth finding a good fit.

Blisters are a sign your shoes don’t fit. Feeling like you can’t possibly do this is a sign your habit doesn’t fit. 

Neither is a sign of anything being wrong with you.
